Repositories for digital reproductions of primary sources:


NB: Many of these sites will have multilingual sources. Some of the sites are run outside of Anglocentric contexts and will have their interfaces and materials based in other languages. Those are noted.

  • American Antiquarian Society
    • "The AAS library today houses the largest and most accessible collection of books, pamphlets, broadsides, newspapers, periodicals, music, and graphic arts material printed through 1876 in what is now the United States, as well as manuscripts and a substantial collection of secondary texts, bibliographies, and digital resources and reference works related to all aspects of American history and culture before the twentieth century." (Retrieved from the "About" page on 30 August 2019)
    • copyright
  • American Philosophical Society
    • "The American Philosophical Society Library is a major national center for research in the history of the sciences, early American history, and Native American ethnography and linguistics." (Retrieved from the "Home Page" on 30 August 2019).
    • copyright
  • French: Archives Nationales d'Outre Mer
    • Papers related to French colonial history dating back to the seventeenth century.
    • copyright
  • French: Bisbliothèque numérique de l'observatoire de Pari
    • Collections related the history of Astronomy.
    • copyright
  • Biodiversity Heritage Library
    • "The Biodiversity Heritage Library improves research methodology by collaboratively making biodiversity literature openly available to the world as part of a global biodiversity community." (Retrieved from the "Home Page" on 30 August 2019).
    • copyright
  • British Museum
    • "The Museum’s collection online offers everyone unparalleled access to objects in the collection. This innovative database is one of the earliest and most extensive online museum search platforms in the world. There are currently 2,335,338 records available, which represent more than 4,000,000 objects. 1,018,471 records have one or more images." (Retrieved from the Collection Search Page on 30 August 2019).
    • copyright
  • David Rumsey
    • "The David Rumsey Map Collection was started over 30 years ago and contains more than 150,000 maps. The collection focuses on rare 16th through 21st century maps of North and South America, as well as maps of the World, Asia, Africa, Europe, and Oceania. The collection includes atlases, wall maps, globes, school geographies, pocket maps, books of exploration, maritime charts, and a variety of cartographic materials including pocket, wall, children's, and manuscript maps. Items range in date from about 1550 to the present." (Retrieved from the about page on 30 August 2019).
    • copyright
  • Dutch: Delpher
    • Collection of Dutch materials dating back to the seventeenth century.
    • copyright
  • Early Dutch Books Online
    • "Early Dutch Books Online gives full-text access to more than 2 million pages in 10,000 books from the Dutch-speaking region from the period 1781-1800." (Retrieved from Home, 31 August 2019).
    • copyright
  • French: Gallica
    • A repository of 100,000s of digital works, mostly French.
    • copyright
  • Hathi Trust Digital Library
    • "HathiTrust is a partnership of academic & research institutions, offering a collection of millions of titles digitized from libraries around the world." (Retrieved from the home page on 31 August 2019).
    • copyright
  • The John Carter Brown Library
    • "The John Carter Brown Library collection of 50,000 rare books (printed before ca. 1825), manuscripts, and 16,000 reference books and secondary sources (printed after ca. 1825) is distinguished in many subject areas. Most well-known, perhaps, are the Library’s extensive holdings in the literature of European exploration and travel in the Western Hemisphere, from the first Latin edition of the Columbus letter of 1493, through nearly all of the contemporary narratives of Spanish, Portuguese, French, Dutch, and English discovery, exploration, and settlement. The John Carter Brown Library collection is entirely distinct from the Brown University Library collection and is 90% accessible online." (Retrieved from home page on 31 August 2019).
    • copyright
  • Library and Archives of Canada
    • "Library and Archives Canada’s (LAC) collection is the shared documentary heritage of all Canadians and spans the entire history of our country. The collection contains materials in all types of formats from across Canada and around the world that are of interest to Canadians." Retrieved from About the Collection on 31 August 2019).
    • copyright
  • Library of Congress
    • "Access online collections: view maps & photographs; read letters, diaries & newspapers; hear personal accounts of events; listen to sound recordings & watch historic films." (Retrieved from Discovery the Library of Congress, 31 August 2019).
    • copyright
  • Linnean Collections
    • "This digital archive of unique material relating to the society's priceless collections of specimens, manuscripts and letters will enable full global access for investigation allowing researchers to rapidly check details of the specimens on-line, including morphological details and written data." (Retrieved from About the Repository, 31 August 2019)
    • copyright
  • German: Münchener DigitalisierungsZentrum
    • "provides one of the largest and fastest growing digital collections in Germany, now comprising more than 900,000 titles available online." (Retrieved from the "MDZ Centre" on 2 September 2019).
    • copyright
  • Spanish: Primeros Libros de las Américas
    • Repository of sources printed in the Americas before 1601.
    • copyright
  • Rijksmueum
    • "The Rijksmuseum’s collection illustrates the history of The Netherlands, from the Middle Ages to the present." (Retrieved from Hightlights, 31 August 2019).
    • copyright
  • Uppsala University Library
    • Breadth of digital resources from the library's archival collection
    • copyright information relayed document by document
  • Virtual Hill Museum & Manuscript Library
    • "vHMML offers resources and tools for the study of manuscripts and currently features manuscript cultures from Europe, Africa, the Middle East, and South Asia. The site houses high-resolution images of manuscripts, many of them digitized as part of HMML’s global mission to preserve and share important, endangered, and inaccessible manuscript collections through digital photography, archiving, and cataloging." (Retrieved from About, 31 August 2019).
    • copyright
  • Wellcome Library
    • "The Library's digital collections cover a wide variety of topics, including asylums, food, sex and sexual health, genetics, public health and war. Published books, pamphlets, archives, posters, photographs, and film and sound recordings are completely free to view." (Retrieved from Digital collections, 31 August 2019).
    • copyright indicated on individual images and artifacts

These are good starting places for sources, but one of the best ways to find the primary source material is to run a search for it. Try adding "digital archive" and "digital collections" to your searches for information on your object of study.
